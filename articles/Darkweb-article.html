Dark Web: Everything you need to know about
the anonymity network

The dark web is a part of the internet that isn't indexed by search engines. You've no doubt heard talk of the "dark web" as a hotbed of criminal activity — and it is. Researchers Daniel Moore and Thomas Rid of King's College in London classified the contents of 2,723 live dark web sites over a five-week period in 2015 and found that 57% host illicit material.
So what's actually is the Dark web? And how does it work? Let's dive in.

Peeling back the layers of the onion

In the 1990s, the US Office of Naval Research (ONR) set out to develop a method for anonymously navigating the internet for espionage purposes. The plan was to set up a network of routers that was separate from the internet’s routers, that could encrypt the traffic, and that only stored the unencrypted IP address of the previous router— meaning all other router addresses along the way were encrypted. The idea was that anyone watching the traffic could not determine the origin or destination of the data. This research became known as “The Onion Router (Tor) Project” in 2002, and it’s now available to anyone to use for relatively safe and anonymous navigation on the web.

How it actually works!

Tor encrypts the data, destination, and sender IP address of each packet. At each hop, the information is encrypted and then decrypted by the next hop when it’s received. In this way, each packet contains information about only the previous hop along the path and not the IP address of the origin. So if someone were to intercept the traffic, they could see only the IP address of the previous hop, and the website owner could see only the IP address of the last router that sent the traffic.

Think about it as want to send a secret message to a friend, but instead of giving it to them directly, you pass it through several trusted couriers. Each courier only knows who handed them the message and who they need to pass it to next—nothing about the original sender or final recipient. This way, if someone intercepts the message at any point, they only see one link in the chain, making it much harder to trace back to you. Remember, it's not impossible but much harder

The Best Place For criminal activity?

The dark web is a haven for illegal activities, including drug trafficking, weapons sales, and human trafficking. It’s also a place where hackers can buy and sell stolen data, such as credit card numbers and personal information. In fact, the dark web is often referred to as the “Wild West” of the internet because of its lawlessness and lack of regulation.

But it’s not all bad. The dark web can also be a place for whistleblowers to share information without fear of retribution and for journalists to communicate with sources in oppressive regimes. It’s a double-edged sword that can be used for both good and evil.

In the dark web, the only problem you will find there that is "dark"—literally dark—is that you can’t easily find a website or information. It’s not that easy; in fact, every website consists of a complex string of 24 letters and numbers, which functions with the Onion Router. This is what actually makes it the dark web!
